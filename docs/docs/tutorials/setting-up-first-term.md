# Setting Up Your First Term

Create your first academic year and term so you have a place to build your course schedule.

## Prerequisites

- The app installed and running

## Step 1: Configure Your Department Name

Before creating any terms, set your department identity. This name appears on every exported schedule, so getting it right now saves editing later.

1. Click **Settings** in the sidebar
2. Under **General**, enter your department name (e.g., "Department of Economics")
3. Under **Academic Year**, confirm the start month is set to **July** (the default, representing a July 1 -- June 30 academic year)

The academic year start month determines how terms are grouped. With a July start, Fall 2025, Spring 2026, and Summer 2026 all belong to academic year "2025-2026."

## Step 2: Create an Academic Year

Academic years are created automatically when you create terms. The app assigns each term to an academic year based on its start date and the start month you configured in Step 1.

For example, if your academic year starts in July:
- A term starting in August 2025 is placed in **AY 2025-2026**
- A term starting in January 2026 is placed in **AY 2025-2026**
- A term starting in June 2026 is placed in **AY 2025-2026**

You do not need to manually create academic years -- they appear automatically as you add terms.

## Step 3: Create Your First Term

Now create a term to hold your course schedule.

1. Click **Terms** in the sidebar
2. Click **+ Add Term** at the top of the page
3. Fill in the details:
   - **Name** -- e.g., "Fall 2025"
   - **Type** -- Select "Fall"
   - **Start Date** -- e.g., September 2, 2025
   - **End Date** -- e.g., December 19, 2025
4. Click **Save**

Your new term appears in the list with a **Draft** status. Draft terms can be freely edited -- you will finalize the term later once the schedule is complete and all conflicts are resolved.

## Step 4: Add Sessions (Summer Terms Only)

If you created a summer term, you will want to add sessions to represent the different sub-periods (Session A, Session B, etc.). Regular Fall and Spring terms typically do not need sessions.

1. Click the **expand arrow** on your summer term row to reveal the sessions area
2. Click **+ Add Session** and fill in:
   - **Name** -- e.g., "A" or "Session A"
   - **Start Date** -- Session start (e.g., June 2, 2025)
   - **End Date** -- Session end (e.g., June 27, 2025)
   - **Head Count Days** -- Days before the head count date
   - **Head Count Date** -- The official enrollment snapshot date
   - **Notes** -- Any additional information
3. Repeat for each session (B, C, D, Full, etc.)

If you have session data in a spreadsheet, use the **Paste Sessions** button to import them all at once. The paste dialog supports tab-separated or comma-separated values and offers Merge mode (adds alongside existing sessions) or Replace mode (removes existing sessions first).

## Step 5: Create Additional Terms

Repeat Step 3 for each term you need in the academic year. A typical setup might include:

- **Fall 2025** (September -- December)
- **Spring 2026** (January -- May)
- **Summer 2026** (June -- August, with sessions)

You can have multiple draft terms open simultaneously, which is useful for planning ahead.

## What You've Learned

- How to set your department name and academic year start month in Settings
- How academic years are automatically assigned based on term start dates
- How to create a term with name, type, and date range
- How to add sessions to summer terms for sub-period tracking
