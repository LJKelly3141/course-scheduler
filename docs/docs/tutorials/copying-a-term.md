# Copying a Term for Next Semester

Duplicate an existing term to get a head start on next semester's schedule -- then adjust sections, instructors, and rooms as needed.

## Prerequisites

- A completed term to use as a starting point (see [Building a Schedule](?page=tutorial-build-schedule))

## Step 1: Find the Source Term

1. Click **Terms** in the sidebar
2. Locate the term you want to copy (e.g., "Fall 2025")

Pick a term that closely resembles what you need for the upcoming semester. If you are building Spring 2026, last year's Spring 2025 is usually the best starting point, though Fall 2025 can work if your Spring offering pattern is similar.

## Step 2: Start the Copy

1. Click the **Copy** button on the source term's row
2. A dialog appears with configuration options

## Step 3: Configure the New Term

Fill in the details for the copy:

- **New Name** -- e.g., "Spring 2026"
- **Type** -- Select the appropriate type (Fall, Spring, Summer, or Winter)
- **Start Date** -- The new term's start date (e.g., January 20, 2026)
- **End Date** -- The new term's end date (e.g., May 15, 2026)
- **Include Assignments** -- Check this to copy instructor and room assignments along with sections. If unchecked, sections are copied but all instructor and room assignments are cleared (set to TBD/unassigned).

Choose whether to include assignments based on your situation:
- **Include assignments** if most instructors and rooms will stay the same and you just need to make a few swaps
- **Exclude assignments** if you are doing a major reshuffle and prefer to start with a clean slate for instructor and room assignments

## Step 4: Review the Copy

After clicking **Copy**, the new term appears in the terms list with Draft status.

1. Click the term name or expand its row to see the copied sections
2. Switch to the **Schedule Grid** and select the new term to see the visual layout
3. Check the **Dashboard** for a summary of what was copied

Everything from the source term is duplicated into the new term: sections, meetings (time slots), and optionally instructor and room assignments.

## Step 5: Make Adjustments

Now tailor the copied schedule for the new semester:

**Swap instructors:**
1. Go to the **Schedule Grid** and click a meeting that needs a different instructor
2. Click **Edit** and select the new instructor
3. Or go to **Courses**, expand a section, and change the instructor assignment there

**Adjust sections:**
- Add new sections for courses with increased demand
- Remove sections that will not run next semester
- Change enrollment caps based on enrollment trends (check **Analytics > Course Detail** for historical data)

**Update rooms:**
- If a room is unavailable next semester, click the meeting and reassign to a different room
- Use the **By Room** view on the Schedule Grid to see room utilization and identify open slots

**Handle time changes:**
- Drag meetings to different time slots on the grid
- Or edit meetings individually to change day patterns or times

After making adjustments, check the **Conflicts** sidebar for any new issues introduced by your changes.

## What You've Learned

- How to copy an existing term to create a new one
- How to choose whether to include instructor and room assignments
- How to review the copied term and adjust sections, instructors, rooms, and times for the new semester
