# Feature Ideas: Department Management Tool

Beyond course scheduling, these features would make the app a comprehensive department management tool for academic department chairs.

## Faculty Management

1. **Faculty teaching preferences** — Let instructors rank which courses they want to teach (not just time availability, but course preferences). Chairs collect these every semester.

2. **Multi-term workload view** — See an instructor's load across fall/spring/summer side-by-side. Chairs need to balance annual workload, not just one term.

3. **Sabbatical / leave tracking** — Track who's on sabbatical, medical leave, or reduced appointment. Affects available teaching capacity.

4. **Office hours tracking** — Chairs often need to know when faculty hold office hours, especially for coverage.

5. **Adjunct/IAS contact & pay info** — Notes field for contract status, pay rate, or hiring status per term.

## Curriculum & Program Planning

6. **Course rotation plan** — A multi-year grid showing which courses are offered which terms. Essential for ensuring students can complete programs on time.

7. **Course prerequisites visualization** — See prerequisite chains so the chair can ensure prereqs are offered before the courses that need them.

8. **Program requirement mapping** — Link courses to degree program requirements (major, minor, gen ed) so the chair can verify all programs are covered each year.

9. **Cross-listed / stacked courses** — Track courses that are cross-listed across departments or lecture+lab pairings.

## Enrollment & Demand

10. **Historical enrollment trends** — Chart enrollment for a course over past terms. Helps decide whether to add/cut sections.

11. **Waitlist / demand tracking** — Track courses that filled up or had waitlists to justify adding sections.

12. **Section cap recommendations** — Based on historical enrollment, suggest appropriate caps for next term.

## Budget & Resources

13. **Adjunct budget tracking** — How many adjunct credits are budgeted vs. assigned. Chairs manage a limited adjunct budget.

14. **Room utilization report** — Which rooms are underused or overbooked across the department's schedule. Useful for space requests.

15. **Lab/equipment scheduling** — Some departments have shared labs or equipment that need scheduling beyond just rooms.

## Communication & Workflow

16. **Notes / annotations on sections** — Free-text notes per section (e.g., "needs smart classroom", "co-taught with Biology", "chair approved overload").

17. **Term planning checklist** — A checklist of tasks for each scheduling cycle: collect preferences, draft schedule, review with dean, finalize, submit to registrar.

18. **Change log / audit trail** — Who changed what and when. Even in a single-user app, seeing "I moved this section on Feb 3" is valuable.

19. **Export to registrar format** — Many registrars need a specific format (Banner, PeopleSoft, etc.) beyond just a printable schedule.

## Reporting

20. **Department summary dashboard** — Total SCH generated, FTE coverage, adjunct vs. faculty credit split, sections per modality — the kind of numbers a dean asks for.

21. **Accreditation data export** — Credit hour distribution, class size averages, faculty qualification mapping — data needed for program reviews and accreditation.

22. **Instructor schedule cards** — A per-instructor printable schedule (their courses, times, rooms) to hand out or email.

## Quality of Life

23. **Semester-to-semester cloning with diff** — Clone last fall's schedule to this fall, then see what changed as you edit. More useful than a blank start.

24. **"What if" sandbox mode** — Duplicate a draft schedule to try alternatives without affecting the working copy.

25. **Deadline / calendar integration** — Key dates: when the schedule is due to the registrar, when room requests close, add/drop deadlines.

## Priority Recommendations

The highest-impact additions for a department chair would be:

- **Course rotation planning** (#6) — chairs spend significant time on this manually
- **Multi-term workload view** (#2) — annual load balancing is a core responsibility
- **Section notes** (#16) — simple but constantly needed
- **Semester cloning** (#23) — most schedules are incremental changes from the prior year
